One hundred days
to the arena

The Main Event is not a poker tournament. It is a two-week war of patience, pressure and stillness — a freezeout marathon where the bubble doesn't arrive until Day 4. You will train as three fighters in one body.

⅓ Gladiator
The Foxen third

Trained violence

Theory-forged, fearless at the highest leverage nodes. A six-bet jam is not recklessness when you have studied the tree. Understand — don't memorize — then strike where others freeze.

⅓ Mad Max
The Deeb third

Wasteland survival

The freezeout is the wasteland: no re-entry, no second rig. Scavenge chips from weak fields, tell believable stories with your aggression, and show up every day — win or lose.

⅓ Zen Monk
The Mateos third

Still water

Feel the emotion and perform anyway. No fixed strategy — adaptive pressure, patient observation, and river calls made because the range says so, not because you want to be right.

Doctrine

Aggressive, but survivalist

The synthesis of Mateos, Foxen and Deeb rejects robotic poker. Know your baselines, know when structure and ICM change them, know who you're playing against, and keep execution stable across long days. Wide exploitative accumulation early; real courage in leverage spots; tighter stack-offs in the freezeout; credible polarized turn-and-river lines; superior sleep and focus.

The Campaign

The hundred days

Five phases of twenty days. Roughly 2.5–4.5 hours a day — scale down 40% or up 50% without breaking the sequence. Every seventh day consolidates; every twentieth is an exam. Check days off as you conquer them — progress is saved on this device.

The Trial

Trial of the three thirds

Eighteen questions drawn from the doctrine — six for the blade, six for the wasteland, six for the stillness. Take it cold on Day 1, again on Day 50, and again on Day 90. The trial tells you which third of you is weakest.

The Vows

Daily non-negotiables

Routine compliance beats a perfect but fragile plan. Every day of the hundred, and every day of the Main Event itself:

Sleep

7–9 hours, every night. The monk guards the temple.

Movement

20–30 min walking or mobility most days; two strength sessions weekly. The gladiator maintains the body.

Water

At wake-up, between study blocks, at every break. In the wasteland, hydration is fuel.

Breath

5–10 minutes of breathing, journaling or visualization. Feel the emotion; perform anyway.

Process

Mark hands, review hands, write one lesson learned. Every single day.
